What is vibe coding?

Vibe coding is a way to write the code for an app by describing to a generative AI tool what you want the app to do and how you want it to look.

Examples (written by Chat GPT):

  1. Interactive teaching tools: quickly generating simple quiz apps, flashcards, or drag-and-drop activities for classrooms.
  2. Rapid prototypes: building small web apps or dashboards to test an idea before investing in full development.
  3. Workflow helpers: making lightweight scripts or utilities (e.g., file renamers, CSV cleaners, or text analyzers) that improve daily productivity.

Define goals

For the app or tool that we make, let's separate any educational goals, research goals, and desired technological features from each other.

What are some possible educational goals of the app we want to make?

  • Adhere to principles of backward design.
  • Review or assess a specific learning objective in a way that activates retrieval and spaced learning.
  • Enable learner to self-assess and identify areas for improvement with regard to a specific learning objective.
  • What else?

What are some possible research goals of the app?

  • Collect data on how many attempts it takes a learner to get the right answers.
  • Collect data on most common incorrect answers and actions within assessment tasks.
  • Gather feedback or reflections about a one-time or repeated experience.
  • Gather data that cannot be represented with text or in a classical survey or quiz format.
  • Track each user action, such as what was clicked on and when or time spent on each task.

What are some possible technological features that we want our app to have?

  • Creative and interactive input or assessment.
  • Provide feedback immediately.
  • Help collect data for quality improvement and research purposes.
  • Allow user data to be exported, saved, downloaded, and/or sent to an instructor or supervisor.
  • Allow instructors or supervisers to receive, interpret, and analyze data from students or trainees.
  • Display key data or information in an interactive dashboard.
  • What else?

Discussion

What questions do you have?

Let's pause to discuss any questions you might have.

What are the risks of vibe coding?

  • App could behave unexpectedly
  • Generative AI could misinterpret your instructions.
  • Information might not be accurate
  • What other risks can you think of?

What are the benefits of vibe coding?

  • Quickly generate interactive learning tools to teach or reinforce learning objectives.
  • Create advanced and aesthetically pleasing interfaces for learners to use, without writing any code manually.
  • What other benefits can you think of?

How can I take this to the next level?

  • Put the app you made online so that others can use it. If you are an educator, you might be able to upload your app to a learning management system for your learners to use. You can also put your app online for free using providers like GitHub.
  • Think bigger and experiment on your own. Example chatbot prompts:
    • I need help making and publishing an interactive app to teach an entire class to my students and assess them.
    • Make an interactive app that students can use to review emergency department protocols, assess their knowledge, and document their progress over time.
    • Make an interactive app that collects data for my research project with a user-friendly and creative interface.
  • Try a dedicated vibe coding platform like Replit, Lovable, Claude Code, or Cursor.
  • Meet with an experienced coder to add features to your app and make it available to potential users.
